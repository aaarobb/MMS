Community Projects

In partnership with Kindred Vanguard and the Houston Multicultural Center, we offer free tutoring and mentorship to 250+ refugee youth throughout the Greater Houston Area.

Around once a month, we gather for a 60-90 minute math session filled with problem sets, games, confidence-building exercises, and shared laughter.

  • Our goal is to create a space of mutual learning, joy, and hope.
  • No grades, no pressure — just fun math activities and quick level-matching.
  • Emphasis on confidence and group connection.

In partnership with Awarenet, we virtually tutor children in the township of Makhanda, South Africa every Monday morning. Having one of the lowest levels of math proficiency in the world, we are eager to help and are currently preparing them for the South African Math Olympiad.

Over time, we have built strong relationships with the students while helping them gradually improve their math skills.

  • This March, our team is traveling to Makhanda, South Africa. In partnership with Awarenet, Rhodes University, and local churches, we will continue our in-person math programs, participate in community service, and create a short documentary on the anti-apartheid movement. The documentary will highlight diverse perspectives through interviews, examine the history of colonialism in South Africa, and explore how these events shaped resistance movements, including those led by the Xhosa people, ultimately influencing the anti-apartheid struggle.

In partnership with Pratham USA, we are raising funds to expand access to quality education for children in rural India.

We support Pratham USA in its mission to expand educational opportunities for children in underprivileged villages across India. We have currently raised upwards of 3,000 dollars.
